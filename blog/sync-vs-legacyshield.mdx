### The Zero-Knowledge Paradox

Zero-knowledge encryption is the gold standard for privacy. It means your "Master Key" (your password) is never stored on the company's servers. If a hacker breaches the data center, they get nothing but encrypted noise.

**The Problem:** If you are the *only* person with the key, and something happens to you, your digital legacy is locked forever. 

In a traditional zero-knowledge setup like Sync.com, your family cannot "reset" your password. They cannot prove their identity to a support agent to gain access. Your most important documents—the ones they need to settle your estate—are effectively deleted the moment you are no longer there to unlock them.
